About Me

I studied computer applications in Lucknow, moved to Noida for my first developer job, and have spent the years since figuring out how to make backend systems survive contact with real users.

I started at Parsh Digital Services writing .NET Framework apps end-to-end — requirements, database design, QA, the whole loop. That grounding in ownership is still how I approach every system I build.

At Chetu India, that turned into architecting microservices platforms on .NET Core and Azure: breaking monoliths apart, wiring up CI/CD, securing secrets in Key Vault, and partnering directly with clients on architecture reviews for teams of six to ten engineers.

What I care about hasn't changed much — clean architecture, defensible decisions, and systems that hold up long after the demo.
